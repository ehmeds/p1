In 2+ sentences describe who you are and what you do.

Hi there! I've never actually written an "About Me" before, so I hope this reads easy and personifies who I am accurately. I'm a crazy geek addicted to learning with a passion for all things technology. I entered geek-hood at the ripe age of 7 when my father bought our first desktop computer. It was a Windows 95 machine and love at first sight! Since then, I've flip flopped through school on what my career would be, while tinkering with every piece of software I could lay my hands on, stoking the fire of my technology passion as a personal hobby. In Dec 2013, Technology finally made an honest gal out of me and I tied the knot with a Bachelor's of Science in Management of Information Systems from the University of Texas.
I'm currently a candidate for the Information Management Master's program here at HES. I traverse the country as a full time Business Analyst Consultant, where I work with Fortune 500 clients learning, refining, and redesigning their business processes, empowering the way they do business with the Microsoft stack. Prior to this I worked extensively off the Linux platform implementing Application Performance Management technologies to help organizations efficiently manage their DevOps cycles.

Why are you taking this course?

Amongst my early youth passions I discovered the glamorous world of web development and have always been intrigued with how websites are designed and developed. As I watched technology progress, my fascination with web technologies grew and I decided to move past just wading in the waters and take the full plunge towards a web development career. I have many ideas I've developed over the years, but lacked the formal skills to bring them to fruition. This course is a great stepping stone on my journey to a front end web development career.

What previous experience do you have with programming?

Formally, I've completed two semesters of Java programming at the University level. This past Spring I took the Fundamentals of Website Development CSCIE-12 course with Professor David Heitmeiyer. In my current job, I do occasional work with HTML/CSS/Javascript.

Are you working on a Mac, PC, or some other platform?

For this course, I primarily work off a Mac running OSX 10.8.5 (I don't like updating, it breaks my software). I have a PC (running Windows 8) with a our environment set up on it as a back up.

Anything else we should know?

I live out of a suitcase most of the week, traveling back and forth from the West coast (where I reside) to the East coast. Dates and deadlines being EST are often hard for me to meet, apologies in advance for slipping on these!
